Veterinary Terminology Glossary

Comprehensive dictionary of veterinary terms with definitions and usage examples

Abdomen

The part of the body between the chest and pelvis, containing the digestive organs.

The veterinarian examined the dog's abdomen for signs of bloating.

Anesthesia

A medical procedure that induces a temporary loss of sensation or consciousness, used during surgery.

The cat was placed under general anesthesia before the surgical procedure.

Bone

The hard, rigid form of connective tissue that makes up the skeleton of vertebrates.

The X-ray revealed a fracture in the dog's leg bone.

Biopsy

The removal and examination of tissue, cells, or fluids from the living body for diagnostic purposes.

A biopsy was performed to determine if the growth was malignant.

Castration

The surgical removal of the testicles in male animals; also called neutering or orchiectomy.

The veterinarian recommended castration to prevent unwanted behaviors and health issues.

Cranial

Relating to or situated near the head or cranium; toward the head end of the body.

The cranial region of the animal showed no signs of trauma.

Distemper

A highly contagious viral disease affecting dogs, characterized by fever, respiratory and neurological symptoms.

Vaccination is essential to protect dogs from distemper.

Endoscopy

A medical procedure using an endoscope (a flexible tube with a camera) to examine internal organs.

The veterinarian used endoscopy to examine the dog's gastrointestinal tract.

Fracture

A break or crack in a bone, usually caused by trauma or disease.

The cat's leg fracture required surgical repair with pins and plates.

Heartworm

A parasitic worm that lives in the heart and blood vessels of infected animals, transmitted by mosquitoes.

Monthly heartworm prevention is recommended for all dogs in endemic areas.

Ovariohysterectomy

Surgical removal of the ovaries and uterus in female animals; commonly called spaying.

The veterinarian performed an ovariohysterectomy to prevent future health issues.

Radiography

The process of taking X-ray images to visualize internal structures of the body.

Radiography revealed a foreign object in the dog's stomach.

Spaying

The surgical removal of the ovaries and uterus in female animals; also called ovariohysterectomy.

Spaying is recommended to prevent unwanted pregnancies and certain diseases.

Ultrasonography

A diagnostic imaging technique using high-frequency sound waves to visualize internal structures.

Ultrasonography was used to examine the cat's abdominal organs.

Ventral

Relating to or situated on the underside or belly side of an animal; opposite of dorsal.

The incision was made on the ventral surface of the abdomen.
